The Economic Impact Of Coffee Industry

Origins 850 AD

Abyssinia, now Ethiopia, is the original home of the coffee (arabica) plant. Kaffa, the province in the south-western highlands where they first blossomed, gave its name to coffee. The formal cultivation and use of coffee as a beverage began early in the 9th century. Prior to that, coffee trees grew wild in the forests of Kaffa, and may in the region were familiar with the berries and the drink.

2015 Quick Facts

  • The total economic impact of the coffee industry in the United States in 2015 was $225.2 billion
  • Coffee-related economic activity comprises approximately 1.6% of the total U.S. gross domestic product
  • Consumers spent $74.2 billion on coffee in 2015
  • The coffee industry is responsible for 1,694,710 jobs in the US economy
  • The coffee industry generates nearly $28 billion in taxes (including ancillary goods)

Starbucks as a Key Player
